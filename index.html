About Me

My name is Ashwin Balaji and I am a rising junior at the University of Pennsylvania, pursuing a BSE and MSE in Computer Science. I’m interested in using my skills at the intersection of finance, machine learning, data science/modeling, software development, and team-based project work. I'm currently a software engineer intern at CME Group, where, using Java and Python, I am working on developing a spread pricing algorithm for options and futures with legs in different price units, as well as building out a latency prediction model to accurately measure latency of time-series orders that enter and exit FALCON, the team's match engine. Previously, I had the opportunity to intern for Siemens Digital, where I added a 'Partner Search' feature to their Integrated Country Planning application, allowing Siemens country planners to connect and reach out to alliance partners for assistance in reselling/delivering products and solutions. I recently was invited to the Susquehana International Group (SIG) software discovery day where I learned about trading and software engineering roles from current executives and employees. I also previously had the ability to conduct Machine Learning research at Michigan State University, where I developed semi-supervised data classification models, such as KNN, Neural Network, SVM, and Random Forest models and compared them across a graph-based semi-supervised algorithm developed by my mentor and her team, a graph-based modification of the classical Merriman Bence Osher scheme. I published a paper in the journal JEI, linked here. I have taken relevant coursework in Discrete Mathematics, Data Structures and Algorithms, Cloud Computing, and Big Data. Outside of school, I am a part of my university's bollywood fusion dance team, Penn Dhamaka, and am an avid percussionist on the South Indian mridangam, having played for the last 10 years of my life.

    We developed a Flight Optimizer web application using Node/SQL for backend, and React/GoogleMaps API for frontend. The application provides users with airline routes for round-trip flights, a hotel and attraction finder, where users can find attractions and filter by attraction type as well as distance from their city, an optimal itinerary planner, where users can enter in up to 3 cities and get the most optimal itinerary, minimizing layovers, as well as a complete trip guideline with attractions and airports along the way.
